Creation of the ARPANET

The steps to start the ARPANET may have been started with Paul Baran and his ideas of packet switching but the person to imagine the ARPANET was J. C. R. Lickider in a research paper which described a "Galatic Network" of interconnected computers1. He wanted to use this system in tandem with other colleges so that they could share research because running tests on large mainframes took time, resources, and money. Lickider worked for the government agency ARPA (hence the name ARPANET) and the government's goal with the project was for the nuclear war scare.
