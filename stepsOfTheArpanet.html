Creation of the ARPANET

By Riley Palomata

The steps to start the ARPANET may have been started with Paul Baran and his ideas of packet switching but the person to imagine the ARPANET was J. C. R. Lickider in a research paper which described a "Galatic Network" of interconnected computers1. Lickider worked for the government agency ARPA (hence the name ARPANET) and the government's goal with the project was for the nuclear war scare. Another individual who joined the ARPA team to work on the ARPANET was Robert Taylor. He joined ARPA in 1965 and in 1966 he became the director of ARPA's Information Processing Techniques Office (IPTO). During his time working at ARPA he saw that each mainframe or computer that IPTO was funding "had its own sense of community and was digitally isolated from the other one2 ." As director he saw that "if ARPA's scattered computers could be linked together, hardware, software, and data could be efficiently pooled among contractors rather than wastefully duplicated2 ."
